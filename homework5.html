hw5 : emotive eye

For this project, I was inspired by an iconic scene from the horror movie, Psycho (1960). In the movie, and without giving spoilers, the woman is frozen in a state of fear at this moment. The hair of the woman’s eyelashes and brow contrasts with the lightness of her skin, eyeball, and iris. I chose to use her eyebrow and the iris as the main elements to explore complementary colors. I looked at movie posters from the time ‘Psycho’ was released, not only to ensure I was not unintentionally plagiarising, but to sample colors from the posters. I observed that bright saturated primary colors were prominently used. With the color picker tool, I saved the blue '#4A6DD9' as a reference point in my color palette to allude to the movie posters I was inspired from.

I centered the eye upright in the center of the artboard. I used the default line style (uniform round) at 1 point. This allowed me to draw the eyelashes. I decided on ‘Profile 5, Charcoal’ at 6 pt for the eyelids because the weight of the line paralleled the highest point of the eyeball.

Labeling the layers and sublayers helped me organize as I changed details. The eyebrow in this piece is the second version that I made. I was having difficulty conveying the fine and bushy eyebrows. Although I did not end up using the first version in my submission, I did not delete it from the dashboard and hid it by unlocking and clicking the eye icon.
